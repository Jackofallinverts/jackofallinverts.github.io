"Budwing Mantis" Parasphendale agrionina

Various instars of P. agrionina examples pictured. Adult males are visibly smaller.

  • Generally bold, voracious eaters, great for beginners
  • Adult females are flightless with short wings, males can fly
  • I have found that my males mature at i9 and females at i10
  • Adult females can lay as many as 6 oothecae
Availability
  • Unsexed i3-i4 Nymphs ($15 each/$40 for 3/$60 for 5)
  • Adult i10 Females ($20 each/$50 for 3)
"Ghost Mantis" Phyllocrania paradoxa

P. paradoxa examples are pictured. As adults the males look similar, and the females can vary in color. Known color variations are brown, beige, yellow/gold, green, grey, and black.

  • Popular mantis, a staple in the community
  • More timid than other beginner species
  • Tend to sway and box the air dramatically, may also play dead
  • Both sexes of this species mature at i8
  • Adult females may lay 10+ oothecae
Availability
  • Female i6 Presub ($17 each)
  • Female i7 Subadult ($19 each)
“Dead Leaf Mantis” Deroplatys gorochovi
  • Mimics the color and pattern of dead leaves
  • Can play dead, and oftentimes will
  • Individuals can be sexed by pronotum shape at i4
  • Females develop a large curved pronotum "shield"
  • Males mature at i7 while females mature at i8
  • Deroplatys sp. can often live for a long time
Availability
  • i3 Nymphs ($30 each/$80 for 3)
“Asian Flower Mantis” Creobroter apicalis
  • A great beginner mantis, very prey driven
  • Lovely color potential, partial flower mimicry
  • Males mature an instar earlier than females
  • Both males and females are capable of flight
Availability
  • i3-i6 Nymphs ($17 each/$45 for 3)
“African Twig Mantis” Popa spurca
  • Looks like a literal stick
  • Often holds out their arms in pose
  • Will play dead if spooked
  • Males mature at i7-i8 and females at i10
Availability
  • i4-i5 Nymphs ($17 each/$30 for 2)
  • i9 Subadult Female ($25 each)

"Greater Waxmoth" Galleria mellonella
  • Great feeders, easy to care for, easy to breed
  • Keep them warm (~85-95°F) with plenty of food to promote quick growth and large moths
  • Cut food early or limit it to a minimum to produce smaller moths
  • Waxworm recipe and caresheet available on my caresheets tab
  • Deluxe cultures contain twice as much food and twice as many moths
Availability
  • Starter Culture ($35 per culture/$60 for 2)

"Northern Walking Stick" Diapheromera femorata ⚠️
  • All non-native phasmids are ILLEGAL! YOU MUST BE IN THEIR NATIVE RANGE!
  • These WILL breed like crazy if you keep them right, they eat lots of oak (Quercus sp.)
  • I mainly raise mine on Northern Red (Q. rubra) and White Oak (Q. alba) but they will eat other species
  • Young nymphs may need their leaves torn or cut to allow them to eat properly
  • Both sexes of adults and several nymphs are pictured
  • All adults sold have been paired and are likely fertile
  • To hatch, eggs will need to be diapaused (chilled) at 40°F or below for a few months
Availability
  • Eggs ($15 for 20/$30 for 50/$55 for 100)
